Calvary Cemetery

1040 Northampton Street · Est. c. 1870s
Holyoke's principal Catholic burial ground, serving Sacred Heart, Our Lady of the Holy Rosary, and Blessed Sacrament parishes. Spans the Civil War through Vietnam.

Forestdale Cemetery

304 Cabot Street · Est. 1860
Holyoke's public secular cemetery, organized in 1860 and dedicated in 1862. Contains GAR plots, a potter's field, and the grave of Medal of Honor recipient John MacKenzie (WWI).

Elmwood Cemetery

2190 Northampton Street · Oldest burial 1755
The oldest burial ground in Holyoke, given to the city in 1955. Home to veterans of the French & Indian War, Revolutionary War, and Civil War through World War II — including two veterans killed in 1777 and a WWI Navy Yeomanette.

St. Jerome Cemetery

125 Saint Jerome Avenue · Est. 1864
Opened in 1864, originally known as the Catholic Cemetery of Holyoke. Served Irish and French Canadian immigrant communities. Eight victims of the 1875 Precious Blood Church fire are buried here.

Rock Valley Road · Some burials from 1700s
A small burial ground in West Holyoke near the Southampton line, with early interments dating to the 18th century. Four soldiers of the Revolution lie here.
